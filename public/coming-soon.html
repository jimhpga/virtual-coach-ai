What’s next for Virtual Coach AI

We’re not guessing. We’re building the stuff golfers actually need: honest feedback, pressure-proof patterns, and coaching you can use without standing on a lesson tee.

Below is what’s about to land, what’s being built, and what we’re testing in the lab.

Launching Soon Upcoming

These features are basically in the chamber. You’ll start seeing them in reports and on your dashboard.

Face Control / Start Line Tracker Soon

We read how “quiet” your clubface is through impact (P7–P8) and tell you how trustworthy your start line really is. No more “felt square, went left.”

  • Red / Yellow / Green face stability
  • “Will this hold up under pressure?” truth rating
  • One fix, not eight YouTube feels

Coach Locker / Player History Soon

For coaches: all your players, all their swings, all their reports — same structure every time, no scrambling.

  • Before / after snapshots
  • Shareable improvement timeline for parents/players
  • Lesson prep in 30 seconds, not 30 minutes

Junior Mode & Parent Report Soon

Kid-friendly feedback for the player, clean bullet points for the parent. Clear, positive, no biomech lecture.

  • Simple checkpoints kids can remember
  • “Here’s what they’re getting better at this week” framing
  • Printable / textable for mom or dad

Pre-Shot / Mental Routine Builder Soon

We’ll attach a simple routine to your swing profile: how you stand in, your “calm swing thought,” and your safe miss under nerves.

  • Your trigger feel at address
  • Your bailout pattern when you tighten up
  • Your reset after a bad swing (without the spiral)

In the shop right now In Development

These are actively being trained, tested with real swings, and tuned so they give useful answers and not just “tech words.”

Side-by-Side Tour Comparison Building

Your P4 next to a vetted tour model P4. Your P6 next to their P6. No influencer posing, just real delivery windows.

  • “Keep this, it’s already tour-quality” callouts
  • “Here’s the one piece that matters” clarity
  • Coach mode for live lessons

Markerless Kinematic Checkpoints Building

K-Vest style sequencing from a normal phone video. No suit, no wires, no dots. We measure how your body is actually moving.

  • Hip–torso separation in transition
  • Upper vs lower body sequencing timing
  • “Are you turning or just thinking you’re turning?” readout

Ground Force / Pressure Use (Markerless) Building

Estimating how you use the ground to create speed — bracing, pushing, rotating — without a force plate in your garage.

  • “You’re jumping early” vs “You’re posting and turning”
  • When in the downswing you’re actually creating speed
  • How to unlock yards without just swinging harder

AI Drill Prescriptions Building

You get drills that match your leak. Not generic “do this drill” content. We’re talking: “Here’s the one feel that fixes your miss.”

  • Driven by the actual issue we flagged in your report
  • Short, direct, coach voice
  • Meant to be done on the range today

On the whiteboard Under Review

These are in testing / research. If they’re good for golfers, they ship. If they’re just “cool tech,” they don’t.

“Will it travel?” Pressure Score Exploring

Range swings lie. Course swings expose you. We’ll call out whether your move will actually hold up on the first tee when people are watching.

  • Hanging back / steering = range-only pattern
  • Posted left / quiet face = course-ready pattern
  • Blunt honesty, not sugar coating

Dispersion & Gapping Map Exploring

Tie ball flight patterns to body patterns. Not just “you missed left,” but “here’s the move that caused the miss.”

  • Your predictable miss, named and owned
  • Setup tweaks to take the big miss out of play
  • Where you’re leaving free yardage in the bag

Voice AI Range Mode Exploring

Hands-free coaching. Hit balls and get real-time reminders in plain English, not robot talk.

  • “Post left sooner” / “Calm the face” type cues
  • Reinforces the 1 priority you’re supposed to be working on
  • No nonsense, no fake hype

Multi-sport engine Exploring

Same engine, different sport: baseball swing, throw mechanics, basketball shooting, etc. (Golf stays first. Golf is home base.)

  • Same “see it / fix it / train it” model
  • Motion first, not gadget first
  • Serious use cases for coaches and parents
We’re not trying to drown you in features. We’re trying to give you the two or three things that will actually lower your score. If something doesn’t help you on the course, it doesn’t make the cut.
