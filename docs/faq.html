A short history of golf instruction — and why AI is the next natural step

Golf coaching started as art and intuition. Then video made the invisible visible. Physics replaced folklore with ball-flight laws and cause/effect. Wearables tracked tempo, load, and patterns so practice became measurable. Frequently Asked Questions

How should I film my swing for the best results?
Face-on or down-the-line both work great. Quick checklist:
  • Camera at roughly hip height, about 8–12 feet away.
  • Keep the entire club and hands in the frame at all times.
  • Level horizon; lock focus/exposure if possible.
  • 60–120fps helps, but standard phone video is fine.
  • Steady mount (tripod, bag clip, or a friend with still hands).
Down-the-line: set the lens on the target line, just outside the hands. Face-on: square to the stance line.
What’s inside the report?
Coach-ready breakdown:
  • P1–P9 with GOOD / OK / NEEDS HELP tags.
  • Position & swing consistency bars (tempo ratio, release timing, windows).
  • Power Score summary and deltas.
  • Top 3 Fundamentals, Top 3 Power Errors, Top 3 Quick Fixes, plus drills.
  • 14-day practice plan concept to turn fixes into habits.
Does this replace my coach?
No—Virtual Coach AI amplifies great coaching. Share reports, save lesson time for higher-value work, and keep accountability between lessons. Coaches can use our reports as a clean, common language.
