Quick checklist (best results)

Camera height: hip-high Distance: ~8–10 ft Orientation: landscape Frame: feet → clubhead Lighting: sun behind camera Stability: tripod or bag

Face-On (FO)

  • ✓
    Stand the camera **across** from your lead hip, about hip height (belt level), ~8–10 feet away.
  • ✓
    Center your **chest** in frame; include feet at setup and the **full clubhead** at the top/finish.
  • ✓
    Keep the lens **level**; set phone to landscape. Use a tripod or lean on your bag for stability.
  • ×
    Don’t shoot from knee or eye level—angle distortion makes posture and arm angles hard to read.

Great for: grip, posture, pressure shift, shaft lean at impact, and finish balance.

Down-the-Line (DTL)

  • ✓
    Set the camera **on the hand line** (between feet and ball), hip-high, ~8–10 ft behind you.
  • ✓
    Aim straight down the **target line**; keep the ball and clubhead visible through the whole swing.
  • ✓
    Landscape orientation; keep horizon level to avoid fake “flat/steep” reads.
  • ×
    Don’t set the camera too far inside your feet or directly behind the ball—both skew swing-plane visuals.

Great for: club path, face control, shaft pitch (P2/P6), and alignment.

Keep the whole club

Frame **feet to clubhead**. At the top (P4) and finish (P9), the head shouldn’t leave the screen.

Lighting & focus

Best when the **light is behind the camera**. Tap-to-focus on your chest; avoid heavy backlight.

Playback speed

Normal speed is perfect. 60fps is nice; super slow-mo is optional and not required.
